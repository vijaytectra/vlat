About VLAT (Vinayaka Mission's Law Admission Test)

The Vinayaka Mission's Law Admission Test (VLAT) is the official entrance examination conducted by Vinayaka Mission's Law School (VMLS) to assess the aptitude and preparedness of students seeking admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate law programmes. VLAT evaluates key skills essential for a successful legal career and provides a fair, transparent, and student-friendly pathway for law aspirants across the country.

Reading comprehension
Logical reasoning
Critical thinking

VLAT is designed to be a smooth, stress-free experience for students who wish to pursue law. With multiple test slots, an online format, and no negative marking, the exam ensures every student has the best opportunity to showcase their potential without unnecessary pressure.

Why is VLAT Conducted?

Vinayaka Mission's Law School (VMLS) recognizes that the legal profession requires not just
knowledge of law, but also strong analytical abilities, critical thinking, and effective
communication skills. VLAT is specifically designed to evaluate whether an applicant possesses
the intellectual foundation necessary to excel in legal studies and the legal profession.

Instead of relying solely on board marks or subjective criteria, VLAT offers a merit-based,
standardized way to identify motivated and capable students. The exam ensures that admission
to VMLS is fair, accessible, and aligned with the school's commitment to academic excellence.

VLAT Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for VLAT, candidates must meet specific educational qualifications and minimum marks requirements based on their chosen programme and category.

1. For 5-Year Integrated Programmes (BA LL.B., B.Com. LL.B., BBA LL.B.)

Educational Qualification

Candidates must have passed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination from a recognized board.

Minimum Marks Required:

General Category 45%
OBC (Non-Creamy Layer) 42%
SC/ST/PWD 40%

2. For 3-Year LL.B. Programme

Educational Qualification

Candidates must have completed a Bachelor's degree (in any discipline) from a recognized university.

Minimum Marks Required:

General Category 45%
OBC (Non-Creamy Layer) 42%
SC/ST/PWD 40%

3. For LL.M. Programme

Educational Qualification

Candidates must hold UG law degree from recognized university.

Minimum Marks Required:

General Category 50%
OBC (Non-Creamy Layer) 47%
SC/ST/PWD 45%

Important Notes

Candidates appearing in their final year examination are also eligible to apply, subject to meeting the minimum marks criteria at the time of admission.

Age limit and other specific requirements (if any) will be clearly mentioned in the official VLAT admission notification.

Reservation policies follow government norms and are subject to change as per updated regulations.
